Work experience

Nearly two decades in the culinary industry has brought me into some of the most dynamic and exciting kitchens Canada has to offer. From Alberta all the way to Nova Scotia I have been honing my craft and learning every facet of the kitchen industry. Along the way I have picked up many habits and skills that lend themselves well to working in IT/web development. Being organized and able to streamline work flow, Working under constant deadlines with a sense of urgency and the ability to juggle many moving parts at once are just some examples of the essential qualities my career in kitchens has instilled in me. I am eager for the opportunity to apply those skills in a new field and excited at where they will continue to take me. Listed here are a few of the highlights of my last eighteen years.

Department of National Defense. 2019-2023

I spent four years within a civilian capacity, employed by the DND at CFB Halifax. There I worked with a mixed team of civilian and Navy members to ensure all the proper nutritional needs of military members stationed in our building were met. From meal planning and prep to the final cooking and serving of the meals, our team served up an entire buffet three times daily.

Another fulfilling aspect of my position with the DND was making sure new recruits received the proper training they needed to succeed in the culinary trade. We had a constant cycle of new cooks at various stages in their education that we got to work alongside to ensure they had the skills needed to keep our Navy fed on boats and bases all across Canada once they were fully certified in their trade.

Durty Nelly's Irish Pub. 2016-2019

I served as a Line Cook and Prep Cook at Durty Nelly's in Halifax, NS, where I played a crucial role in the kitchen's day-to-day operations. As a Line Cook, I led the team in executing culinary tasks on the hot line, ensuring the timely and high-quality preparation of menu items. My responsibilities extended to meticulous prep cooking, where I followed standardized recipes and maintained a clean and organized workspace. Beyond the kitchen, I took charge of inventory management, monitoring and controlling stock levels while minimizing waste. Additionally, I actively participated in event catering, collaborating with the team to deliver seamless service during various events. My commitment to quality control, adaptability to changing priorities, and effective communication within the team contributed to the establishment's reputation for consistently excellent food. This experience not only refined my culinary skills but also strengthened my ability to thrive in a fast-paced and collaborative kitchen environment.

Little Oak Wine bar. 2015-2016

I proudly served as the Chef de Partie at Little Oak, a sophisticated wine bar nestled in the heart of Halifax, NS. In this role, I oversaw a dynamic and ever-changing culinary experience, managing a small but diverse menu of 7 to 10 items that rotated weekly based on seasonal availability. As Chef de Partie, I collaborated closely with the culinary team to curate a menu that showcased the best and freshest ingredients, ensuring an elevated and memorable dining experience for our patrons. The intimate size of the menu allowed for meticulous attention to detail in crafting each dish, and my role involved not only execution but also contributing creatively to menu development. Little Oak's commitment to seasonal, high-quality ingredients and a regularly evolving menu provided an exciting platform for culinary innovation, and my tenure as Chef de Partie allowed me to play a key role in shaping and delivering this distinctive gastronomic experience.
